# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Hiring managers and design leads at high-paying tech companies, scanning portfolios quickly (30 seconds or less). They are evaluating candidates for senior, principal, and staff UX designer or UX engineer roles. They skim on laptops during working hours, often with multiple tabs open, and decide within a few scrolls whether to dig deeper or move on.

## Product Purpose

A portfolio site for a Senior UX Designer at Amazon. The site itself is a portfolio piece: it demonstrates technical skill, design taste, and AI fluency through its own craft. It must communicate seniority, systems thinking, and shipped impact fast enough that a hiring manager gets the full picture in a few scrolls. The primary action is clicking into a case study for proof. Resume download and contact are secondary but always accessible.

## Brand Personality

Precise, confident, fun. The tone of someone who takes their craft seriously but doesn't take themselves too seriously. Like a well-designed developer tool's marketing page that also has personality.

## Anti-references

- Generic template aesthetics: effects or layouts that look like they were dropped in without thought, regardless of how popular the style is
- SaaS landing page energy: gradient blobs, Lottie animations, hero-metric templates
- Beauty-first portfolios that reveal no evidence of strategic thinking
- Process theater: showing a generic double-diamond without connecting it to actual decisions
- Walls of text without visual hierarchy
- AI slop: designs that scream "AI made this" without craft or intention

## Visual Direction

Ancient cartography meets modern technology. The site feels like a modern interface on aged paper: warm parchment substrate with SVG grain noise, faint pencil-weight construction lines (ruled grids, compass arcs, measurement ticks, serif annotations), and edge vignette. Reference: Henry David Thoreau's Kalendar book. All texture at 3-6% opacity, felt more than seen. Dark mode shifts to aged dark leather quality. The contrast between old material and precise modern layout is what makes it work.

## Design Principles

1. **Scanability over storytelling.** Every viewport delivers value. Dense information, not dense text. Visual hierarchy does the work before anyone reads a word.
2. **Restraint makes bold moments land.** 90% clean and structured, 10% ambitious and surprising. The quiet parts are what make the wow zones feel earned.
3. **Show the thinking, not the process.** Demonstrate design decisions through interactive artifacts, not paragraphs about methodology. The site itself is evidence.
4. **Fun and insightful.** The site should leave hiring managers feeling "this person would be fun and insightful to work with, I need to talk to her in person."
5. **Craft is the credential.** With limited product visuals, the site's own motion, typography, and interaction design carry the portfolio. Every detail is intentional.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG 2.1 Level A compliance
- `prefers-reduced-motion: reduce` disables all animation; site must be fully functional and visually complete as a static layout
- Every hover state has a touch equivalent
- `font-display: swap` for any custom fonts
